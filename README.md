# -Global-Automotive-Sales-Analytics-2018-2025-
A data analysis project covering global automotive sales performance across 4 regions, 8 models, and 8 years — focused on regional revenue balance, model-level economics, and the EV/BEV transition.
📌 Overview

This project analyzes automotive sales data to answer four core business questions:
Which regions and models drive the most revenue?
Where is volume concentrated vs. where is value concentrated?
How fast is the EV (BEV) transition happening, and is it consistent across markets?
What macro factors (GDP, fuel prices) are influencing the shift to EVs?



🔑 Key Findings

Regional Performance
Revenue is well-balanced globally — all four regions (China, Rest of World, USA, Europe) fall within ~3% of each other (24.75%–25.57% share)
China leads on both total revenue (₹401.75B cumulative) and average unit economics


Model Economics
X7 is the #1 revenue model in every single region — highest in Europe at ₹75.46B — pointing to universal demand rather than a regional preference
X7 also commands the highest average price of any model (₹745.9M avg), while iX and i4 (electric models) lead in unit volume (3.14M and 3.13M units)
This is the core volume-vs-value split: EVs sell the most units, but the flagship X7 generates the most revenue per unit


Revenue Growth
Total revenue grew +22% over 8 years: ₹176.8B (2018) → ₹215.6B (2025)
Growth was flat in 2018–19, then jumped +11% in 2020, followed by steady growth every year since


EV Transition
BEV mix grew from 2.35% (2018) to 22.35% (2025) — nearly a 10× increase, and the most consistent trend in the dataset across all four regions
Already inside the 20–25% target range as of 2025


Macro Backdrop
GDP growth held steady at 2.9%–3.2% throughout the period
Fuel price index rose +35% since 2018
Flat GDP + rising fuel costs is a plausible structural tailwind behind EV adoption and rising average prices


Volatility Warning
Annual growth trends mask large month-to-month swings — as much as +417% (USA/X3, Dec 2020) to −81% (China/X7, Apr 2025)
Recommendation: base inventory and supply planning on 3-month rolling averages, not single-month figures
